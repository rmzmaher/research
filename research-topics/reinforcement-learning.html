Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning for Enhanced Oil Recovery

Overview

Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL) represents a cutting-edge approach to optimizing complex, distributed systems like oil reservoirs. In this framework, each well or production unit acts as an intelligent agent that learns optimal control strategies through interaction with the environment, while coordinating with other agents to maximize overall system performance.

Research Objectives

  • Develop decentralized control strategies for multi-well production systems
  • Optimize waterflooding operations through coordinated agent actions
  • Maximize oil recovery while minimizing operational costs
  • Enable real-time adaptive control in response to reservoir dynamics
  • Handle complex multi-objective optimization problems (production, cost, environmental impact)

Mathematical Framework

Our MARL framework employs several advanced techniques with rigorous mathematical foundations:

1. Decentralized Actor-Centralized Critic (DACC) Architecture

The DACC framework combines the benefits of decentralized execution with centralized training. Each agent i has:

Actor Network (Decentralized):

πθi(ai|si)

Critic Network (Centralized):

Qφ(s, a1, ..., aN)

where s is the global state, ai is the action of agent i, and N is the number of agents.

2. Policy Gradient with Physics-Informed Rewards

The objective function combines production optimization with physics constraints:

J(θ) = Eτ~πθ[Σtγtrt]

rt = rproduction + λphysicsrphysics - λcostct

where rproduction rewards oil production, rphysics enforces mass balance and Darcy's law, and ct penalizes operational costs.

3. Actor-Critic Update Rules

The actor and critic networks are updated using:

Critic Update:

LQ = E[(Qφ(s, a) - y)2]

y = r + γQφ'(s', a')

Actor Update:

∇θiJ ≈ E[∇θilog πθi(ai|si) · A(s, a)]

where A(s, a) = Q(s, a) - V(s) is the advantage function.

4. Physics-Informed Constraints

The reward function incorporates reservoir physics through penalty terms:

  • Mass Balance: Σαqα = 0 (conservation of mass)
  • Darcy's Law: vα = -(krαk/μα)∇pα
  • Pressure Constraints: pmin ≤ p ≤ pmax
  • Injection Limits: qinj ≤ qmax

Research Outcomes

Waterflooding Optimization (Franklin Open 2025)

  • Pioneered new MARL algorithm improving recovery efficiency by 15% compared to conventional methods
  • Achieved 92% accuracy in flow prediction using physics-informed neural networks
  • Reduced computational time by 75% compared to traditional optimization methods
  • Successfully demonstrated on synthetic and field-scale reservoir models

Steam Injection Optimization (Neural Computing & Applications 2023)

  • Actor-critic RL framework for steam injection optimization in thermal EOR
  • Achieved 30% cost reduction in field trials compared to conventional methods
  • Optimized steam-to-oil ratio (SOR) while maintaining production targets
  • Real-time decision-making with sub-minute response times
