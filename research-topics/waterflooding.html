Waterflooding Optimization Using Physics-Informed Reinforcement Learning

Overview

Waterflooding is one of the most widely used enhanced oil recovery (EOR) methods, involving the injection of water into oil reservoirs to maintain pressure and displace oil toward production wells. Optimizing waterflooding operations requires determining optimal injection rates, well locations, and injection patterns to maximize oil recovery while minimizing water production and operational costs. This research combines physics-informed neural networks with multi-agent reinforcement learning to achieve optimal waterflooding strategies.

Mathematical Formulation

The waterflooding optimization problem can be formulated as:

Objective Function:

max J = Σt [qo(t) - cwqw(t) - cinjqinj(t)]

Subject to:

Mass Balance: ∂(φSw)/∂t + ∇·vw = qinj

Darcy's Law: vw = -(krwk/μw)∇(p - ρwgh)

Constraints: qinj,min ≤ qinj ≤ qinj,max, pmin ≤ p ≤ pmax

Physics-Informed Neural Network Component

The PINN component ensures physical consistency by incorporating conservation laws into the loss function:

  • Mass Conservation Residual: Enforces continuity equation for water phase
  • Darcy's Law Residual: Ensures velocity-pressure relationships follow Darcy's law
  • Boundary Conditions: Injection and production constraints
  • Initial Conditions: Reservoir state at t=0

Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning

Each injection well acts as an agent learning optimal injection strategies:

  • State Space: Pressure, saturation, production rates, cumulative recovery
  • Action Space: Injection rates for each well
  • Reward Function: Combines oil production, water production penalty, and physics constraints
  • Coordination: Centralized critic provides global information to decentralized actors

Research Outcomes

  • Achieved 15% improvement in recovery efficiency compared to conventional methods
  • Reduced computational time by 75% using PINNs vs. traditional numerical simulation
  • Published in Franklin Open (Impact Factor: 4.2)
  • Demonstrated scalability to large fields with 100+ wells
  • Successfully validated on both synthetic and field-scale reservoir models
