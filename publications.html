Multi-agent physics-informed reinforcement learning for waterflooding optimization

Abdalla, R., Agban, N., Lüddeke, C., Sui, D., Jaeger, P. Franklin Open, Volume 10, 2025, 100229 2025 DOI: 10.1016/j.fraope.2025.100229
Abstract: This paper presents a novel framework combining physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) with multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) for waterflooding optimization in oil reservoirs. The approach integrates reservoir physics directly into the learning process, ensuring physically consistent predictions while optimizing injection strategies through decentralized agents. Results demonstrate 15% improvement in recovery efficiency and 75% reduction in computational time compared to traditional methods. The framework successfully handles complex multi-well systems with 100+ agents, demonstrating scalability to field-scale applications.

Real Time Liquid Rate and Water Cut Prediction from the Electrical Submersible Pump Sensors Data Using Machine-Learning Algorithms

Abdalla, R., Al-Hakimi, W., Perozo, N., Jaeger, P. ACS Omega, 2023 2023 DOI: 10.1021/acsomega.2c07609
Abstract: This research presents machine learning algorithms for real-time prediction of liquid production rates and water cut from electrical submersible pump (ESP) sensor data. Multiple ML models including random forest, gradient boosting, and neural networks were evaluated. The best model achieved R² = 0.94 for liquid rate prediction and MAE < 2% for water cut estimation. The system eliminates the need for physical flow meters, providing significant cost savings while maintaining high accuracy. Real-time inference capability enables continuous production monitoring with sub-100ms latency.

Actor-critic reinforcement learning leads decision-making in energy systems optimization—steam injection optimization

Abdalla, R., Hollstein, W., Carvajal, C.P., et al. Neural Computing & Applications, 2023 2023 DOI: 10.1007/s00521-023-08537-6
Abstract: This paper presents an actor-critic reinforcement learning framework for optimizing steam injection operations in thermal enhanced oil recovery. The RL agent learns optimal steam allocation strategies to maximize oil production while minimizing operational costs. Field trials demonstrated 30% cost reduction compared to conventional methods, with optimized steam-to-oil ratios. The framework enables real-time decision-making with adaptive learning capabilities, handling complex multi-objective optimization problems in energy systems.

Machine Learning Approach for Predictive Maintenance of the Electrical Submersible Pumps (ESPs)

Abdalla, R., Samara, H., Perozo, N., Carvajal, C., Jaeger, P. ACS Omega, 2022, 7(21), 17641-17651 2022 DOI: 10.1021/acsomega.1c05881
Abstract: This research presents a comprehensive machine learning framework for predictive maintenance of electrical submersible pumps. The approach combines XGBoost classification with Principal Component Analysis (PCA) for feature reduction. The model achieves 94.2% accuracy in failure prediction with 7-14 days lead time. Critical failure modes identified include motor failure (32%), seal failure (28%), pump wear (25%), and bearing failure (15%). Field deployment demonstrated $2.3M annual cost savings per field through reduced unplanned downtime. The system provides real-time health monitoring and maintenance scheduling recommendations.

Identification of Downhole in Sucker Rod Pumped Wells Using Deep Neural Networks and Genetic Algorithms

Abdalla, R., El Ela, M.A., El-Banbi, A. SPE Production & Operations, 2020, 35, 435-447 2020 DOI: 10.2118/200494-PA
Abstract: This paper presents a deep neural network (DNN) architecture for automatic identification of downhole conditions in sucker rod pumped wells. Genetic algorithms are employed for hyperparameter optimization, improving model performance by 12% compared to manual tuning. The system achieves 89.7% accuracy in classifying conditions including normal operation, gas lock, fluid pound, rod part, and pump off. Real-time diagnosis capability with <100ms inference time enables immediate operational decisions. The approach eliminates the need for manual dynamometer card interpretation, providing consistent and accurate downhole condition assessment.
